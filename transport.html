Transport

Public transport

In London, public transport helps people move around easily. The Underground, also called the Tube, is a network of trains that go under the city. Buses run on the roads and can take you almost anywhere.

Overground trains connect different parts of the city, and regular trains can take you to places outside London. To save money, you can use an Oyster card or contactless payment on public transport.

Click here to find how to buy and top-up Oyter card.

These cards work on the Tube, buses, Overground, and some trains, and they often offer the cheapest fares for your travels.

Don’t forget to ‘tap in’ on the bus, and ‘tap in’ and ‘tap out’ on the oveground and underground trains. Look for this sign
