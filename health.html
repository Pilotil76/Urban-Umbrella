Health (NHS, GP , Pharmacies, Dentist)

In the United Kingdom, including London, healthcare is provided by the National Health Service (NHS), which is funded through taxes. When you need medical help, you usually start by registering with a local general practitioner (GP) clinic.

The GP is your main point of contact for non-emergency health issues. If you have a more urgent problem, you can go to the Accident & Emergency (A&E) department at a hospital. The best part is, most healthcare services are free at the point of use, so you don't need to worry about paying each time you see a doctor. Just make sure to carry your NHS card and remember, preventive care is encouraged, so don't hesitate to seek help wheit!
